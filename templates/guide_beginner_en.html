Beginner's Guide to Anime

Last updated: July 19, 2026

"I want to get into anime, but there's too much out there to know where to start" is one of the most common things new fans say. This page walks through a few genres with a representative title each, to help you find the right first watch.

If you want action and battles

For a classic shonen experience, titles like Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba and Jujutsu Kaisen are great entry points, with well-paced battles and an easy-to-follow growth story. Both are also split into clear seasons, which makes for natural stopping points if you're new to the format.

If you want a slower, more reflective story

If you'd rather skip flashy fights in favor of carefully drawn human drama, Frieren: Beyond Journey's End is a quiet, contemplative series with a strong sense of closure in nearly every episode — easy to pick up in small doses.

If you want something light and funny

For a low-pressure watch, comedy-driven titles like SPY x FAMILY are a great fit. The fast, character-driven humor makes it approachable even if you've never watched anime before.

If you like mysteries and puzzles

For mystery fans, long-running detective series like Detective Conan (Case Closed) are a safe bet — many episodes are self-contained, so you can jump in almost anywhere.

Still not sure? Start short

If you can't decide on a genre, try a shorter series first (around 12 episodes, one "cour"). Every DOKOMIRU title page lists the episode count clearly, so it's easy to commit to just one season before deciding whether to continue.

Once you find something that catches your eye, search for it by name on the DOKOMIRU homepage to instantly see every legal streaming service that carries it.
